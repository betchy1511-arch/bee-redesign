What We Know About Metairie

Jefferson Parish Housing — What Your Home Is Likely Carrying

Metairie's housing stock is concentrated in two eras:

  • Pre-1960: Older neighborhoods near Old Metairie and Veterans Boulevard — some knob-and-tube, often with service upgrades that were done cheaply
  • 1960s – 1975: The bulk of Jefferson Parish's residential construction — slab foundations, aluminum branch circuit wiring in most homes, 100-amp Federal Pacific or Zinsco panels
  • Post-1985: Copper wiring standard, more modern panels — fewer concerns unless there was DIY work done between then and now

The aluminum wiring issue is specific to Metairie and the surrounding Jefferson Parish communities. Aluminum was used as a cost-cutting measure during the copper shortage of the late 1960s. It expands and contracts differently than copper, which loosens connections over time. The remedy is not a rewire — it's CO/ALR outlet replacement or COPALUM/AlumiConn pigtailing at every device.

If your Metairie home was built between 1965 and 1973, there is a meaningful chance it has aluminum branch circuit wiring. We evaluate it for free as part of any service call. Warm outlets, flickering lights, or breakers that trip for no apparent reason are signals worth following up on.
