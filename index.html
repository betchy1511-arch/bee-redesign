Why Big Easy Electricians

We Know What's Inside
Your Walls

New Orleans housing stock is unlike anywhere else in the country. Pre-1950 shotgun doubles, camelback homes, and 1960s Jefferson Parish construction each present specific wiring challenges that a generic electrician will miss. We've been working inside these walls for three decades.

⚠️

Knob-and-Tube Wiring

Pre-1950 shotgun doubles and camelback homes across Marigny, Bywater, Uptown, and Treme frequently contain active knob-and-tube circuits. Most insurers in Louisiana won't cover homes with K&T. We evaluate, document, and remediate to current NEC standards.

⚡

Aluminum Wiring — Jefferson Parish

1960s-70s construction in Metairie, Kenner, and Harahan typically used single-strand aluminum wiring. Remediation requires CO/ALR devices or COPALUM/AlumiConn pigtailing at every termination — not a generic repair.

📋

Multi-Parish Permit Knowledge

Panel upgrades, new circuits, and full rewires each require permits. We navigate Orleans Parish (City of New Orleans Department of Safety and Permits), Jefferson Parish, and St. Tammany Parish processes — and we pull them correctly the first time.
