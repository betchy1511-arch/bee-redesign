## Brand Positioning Statement

Big Easy Electricians is the electrical contractor that actually knows New Orleans.

Not "licensed and insured" — every competitor says that. What makes BEE different is 30+ years operating in a city with some of the most distinctive and challenging housing stock in America: pre-1950 shotgun doubles still running knob-and-tube wiring, 1960s Jefferson Parish construction loaded with single-strand aluminum, and a permit process that national aggregators have never had to navigate.

**Core position:** The electrician who understands your house — not just electrical theory.

---

## Brand Pillars

### 1. Local Expertise (not generic credentials)
30+ years in New Orleans means knowing the difference between a Marigny camelback built in 1928 and a Kenner ranch built in 1968. The wiring, the risks, and the remediation path are completely different. BEE brings that context to every job.

### 2. Code Knowledge (not just code compliance)
NEC 210.8, NEC 210.12, Orleans Parish permits, Jefferson Parish permits — BEE doesn't just follow the rules, they explain them. Homeowners in older homes need an electrician who can tell them exactly what's required, why, and what happens if it's not done.

### 3. Safety-First Honesty
Not alarmist, not dismissive. If knob-and-tube wiring is fine for now, they'll say so. If it's a fire risk, they'll say that too — and explain specifically why. The brand voice is the electrician who doesn't sugarcoat problems but doesn't manufacture fear either.

### 4. Residential + Commercial Range
Houses, commercial buildings, panel upgrades, full rewires, lightning damage. One contractor for the full scope. No subcontracting to unknown teams.

---

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Expert, honest, locally grounded. Think: the experienced tradesperson who explains things clearly because they respect your intelligence, not because they're selling you something.

**Do:**
- Speak in specifics: "Your 1940 shotgun double likely has knob-and-tube in the ceiling plane" beats "older homes have older wiring."
- Name the code, name the parish, name the outcome.
- Be direct about safety without being alarmist.
- Own the NOLA knowledge angle — it's the clearest differentiator.

**Don't:**
- Generic safety platitudes ("your safety is our priority")
- National-chain formality ("our certified professionals will ensure")
- Vague assurances with no substance behind them
- Anything that could be rewritten with a find-and-replace for any city

**Voice comparison:**
- *Before:* "Energizing Homes and Businesses: New Orleans' Premier Electrical Service Contractor"
- *After:* "30 years rewiring New Orleans — from knob-and-tube shotgun doubles to commercial panel upgrades."
